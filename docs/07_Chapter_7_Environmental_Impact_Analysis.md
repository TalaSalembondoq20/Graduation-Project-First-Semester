    Environmental Impact Analysis

    1.  <span id="_Toc229233551" class="anchor"></span>**Environmental
        Assessment Framework**

In this chapter, the potential environmental impact of the proposed
small-scale waste tire pyrolysis plant will be analyzed. In terms of
environmental assessment, the major effects to be considered are those
related to processing waste tires to generate valuable products,
including pyrolysis oil, carbon black, steel wires, light fuel fraction,
and gas. This is why the waste tire conversion system can be evaluated
as waste-to-resource conversion, where the accumulated waste is reduced
and secondary materials and energy products are generated **(Han et al.,
2023; Wu et al., 2025)**.

The framework for environmental assessment consists in comparing the
proposed pyrolysis technology with the traditional approaches to waste
tire treatment, such as landfilling, open burning, uncontrolled burning,
and tire combustion. The latter two options have more negative
consequences than waste tires recycling or recovery because they involve
higher consumption of new materials, and thus, generate an increased
environmental footprint compared to recycling and recovery **(U.S.
Environmental Protection Agency \[EPA\], 2019)**. It is indicated that,
according to the US EPA Waste Reduction Model, tire recycling
contributes to greater GHG emissions reduction than combustion and
landfilling due to saving energy for virgin secondary material
production.

The proposed approach is to be estimated in terms of environmental
impact related to waste reduction, carbon emissions, avoided emissions,
energy recovery, material recovery, etc. In this case, the processing
capacity of the system amounts to about 10-15 tons per day with a tire
weight equal to 10 kg and 10 tons per batch capacity of the pyrolysis
reactor.

2.  <span id="_Toc229233552" class="anchor"></span>**Carbon Emissions
    Estimation**

The CO2 emissions from the plant depend on reactor heating, electrical
energy, material transport, and machinery operations. The environmental
accounting system often calculates emissions by using a simple equation
where emissions are equal to activity levels multiplied by emission
factors. Thus, the formula for estimating carbon emissions is:

$$CO_{2}\ Emissions = Activity\ Data \times Emission\ Factor
$$

For instance, if the fuel used as the external source is diesel, then
the daily carbon emissions can be calculated by multiplying the amount
of diesel fuel with the factor for emissions of diesel. Nevertheless,
waste tire pyrolysis technology has the environmental benefit since the
gas emitted at this stage can be reused as a source of heat energy for
the reactor. Thus, the energy efficiency of this process is increased
significantly **(Han et al., 2023; Wu et al., 2025)**.

In the proposed system, there are several sources of emissions that
should be analyzed to calculate the environmental benefits. These
include reactor heating, electricity use, pre-processing equipment,
transportation of materials and products. The reactor is considered the
most significant in terms of emissions since it works continuously and
consumes high amounts of energy.

3.  <span id="_Toc229233553" class="anchor"></span>**Carbon Savings and
    Avoided Emissions**

Carbon emissions reduction takes place through the utilization of
pyrolysis outputs as a substitute for traditional materials or through
the redirection of waste tires from environmentally unfriendly disposal
channels. For this project, the key avoided emission opportunities
include avoidance of waste tires landfills, substitution of a fraction
of fossil-derived fuels by pyrolysis liquids, substitution of virgin
carbon black by recycled carbon black, and recovery of steel wires
rather than just using new steel extraction.

According to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), tire recycling
yields higher carbon emissions mitigation benefits than combustion or
disposal in landfills due to lower energy use for the manufacture of
secondary products **(EPA, 2019)**. This strengthens the environmental
rationale of the suggested project since waste tires are used not as
waste, but as raw materials for producing recyclable materials.

4.  <span id="_Toc229233554" class="anchor"></span>**Energy Recovery
    Impact**

Energy recovery is one of the most important environmental benefits of
waste tire pyrolysis. The process produces pyrolysis oil and
non-condensable gas, both of which have energy value. The gas fraction
can be reused internally to support reactor heating, while pyrolysis oil
can be used as an industrial fuel or upgraded into a diesel-like
product.

According to **Han et al. (2023)**, waste tire pyrolysis produces
several useful products, including oil, gas, black carbon, and steel
wire. From an environmental perspective, the reuse of gas is
particularly important because it reduces external fuel demand and
improves thermal efficiency. **Wu et al. (2025)** also discusses the
carbon mitigation potential of waste tire pyrolysis from a life cycle
perspective, especially when recovered products substitute conventional
fuels and materials.

In this project, energy recovery contributes to environmental
performance through three pathways:

<span id="_Toc229236279" class="anchor"></span>**Table 23: Energy
recovery contributes to environmental performance through three
pathways.**

| Energy Recovery Pathway | Environmental Contribution                        |
|-------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Reuse of pyrolysis gas  | Reduces external fuel consumption                 |
| Use of pyrolysis oil    | Provides alternative industrial fuel              |
| Heat integration        | Improves process efficiency and reduces emissions |

Therefore, energy recovery improves both the environmental and economic
performance of the system.

5.  <span id="_Toc229233555" class="anchor"></span>**Sustainability
    Assessment**

Sustainability in the suggested pyrolysis plant can be examined through
its environmental, economic, and operational aspects. Environmentally,
it will address the issue of accumulating waste tires and transform them
into valuable products. Economically, the plant generates revenue from
several sources, including pyrolysis oil, carbon black, steel wires, and
fuel fraction. From an operational standpoint, the system requires
dependable reactor operation and appropriate management of
preprocessing, batching, and product separation.

Tires are regarded as one of the significant environmental issues, owing
to their non-degradable nature and the potential dangers associated with
their disposal, such as fire hazards, storage risks, and pollution.
Pyrolysis is extensively covered in scholarly articles as a potentially
viable thermally driven method since it transforms waste tires into
energy and material products **(Han et al., 2023; Nkosi et al., 2020)**.

The sustainability assessment is summarized below:

6.  <span id="_Toc229233556" class="anchor"></span>**Environmental
    Trade-offs**

Although waste tire pyrolysis provides several environmental benefits,
it also has environmental trade-offs. First, the process requires high
temperature, which means that energy consumption can be significant if
the plant depends heavily on external fossil fuel. Second, pyrolysis oil
may contain sulfur and other compounds that require upgrading before use
in sensitive applications. Third, recovered carbon black may need
further treatment before replacing virgin carbon black in high-value
applications. Therefore, product quality and emission control are
essential for achieving strong environmental performance **(Han et al.,
2023; Wu et al., 2025)**.

Another important trade-off is that the pyrolysis plant must be operated
under controlled industrial conditions. Without proper gas cleaning,
condensation, odor control, and safety systems, the environmental
benefit may decrease. Therefore, the proposed project should include
emission treatment, environmental permits, product testing, and
continuous monitoring.

| Reuses pyrolysis gas            | External energy may still be needed      |

7.  <span id="_Toc229233557" class="anchor"></span>**Discussion of
    Environmental Results**

The environmental analysis indicates that the proposed waste tire
pyrolysis plant has strong environmental potential when properly
designed and controlled. The main benefit is the conversion of waste
tires into useful products instead of sending them to landfills, open
dumping areas, or uncontrolled burning. This supports circular economy
principles and reduces the environmental burden associated with
end-of-life tires.

The system also provides material and energy recovery benefits.
Pyrolysis oil and gas can contribute to energy recovery, while recovered
carbon black and steel wires can reduce the need for virgin raw
materials. These benefits are consistent with previous studies showing
that waste tire pyrolysis can be a feasible waste-to-resource pathway
when environmental control, product upgrading, and stable markets are
considered (Han et al., 2023; Nkosi et al., 2020; Wu et al., 2025).

However, the final environmental performance depends on practical
implementation. The plant must include emission control systems, safe
operation procedures, product quality testing, and proper handling of
sulfur-containing compounds. Therefore, the project can be considered
environmentally promising, but only if it is implemented with adequate
environmental safeguards and regulatory compliance.
