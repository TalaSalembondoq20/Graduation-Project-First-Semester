# 10 Chapter 10 Conclusion and Recommendations

10. <span id="_Toc229233563" class="anchor"></span>Chapter 10:
    Conclusion and Recommendations

    1.  <span id="_Toc229233564" class="anchor"></span>**Summary of
        Findings**

**In this study,** the technical and economic feasibility of the
small-scale tyre pyrolysis process has been assessed using an
integrative approach consisting of discrete-event simulation,
feasibility analysis, and environmental assessment. The discrete event
simulation model was built to simulate the different phases of the
process, which include tyre receiving, deveaning, cutting, shredding,
buffer storage, batching, pyrolysis, and product separation.

**According to the simulation results,** the pre-processing phases of
the process will work effectively and supply materials continuously to
the batching phase. In addition, it has been found out that the main
bottleneck of the process is the pyrolysis phase since it is operated
using batch method and requires a relatively long process time of about
18–23 h per batch. The batch size of the reactor equals 10 tons, which
results in only one batch being processed for one day.

**Based on the economic analysis carried out,** it can be concluded that
the studied project may have good financial feasibility since according
to the calculations the initial capital investment will equal 700,000
JD, the daily net income will amount to about 2,217 JD/day, while the
annual net income will be approximately 798,120 JD. Furthermore, the
expected payback period will be 0.88 years, and the ROI will be about
114%.

**It was shown by environmental analysis** that the process under
investigation can contribute to sustainable waste management due to
transformation of waste tyres into useful products including pyrolysis
oil, carbon black, gas, light fuel fraction, and steel wires.

2.  <span dir="rtl"><span id="_Toc229233565"
    class="anchor"></span></span>**Key Contributions**

One contribution of this study is the development of an evaluation
framework for small-scale waste tyre pyrolysis system. Unlike most
studies that consider the project's feasibility based on financial or
technical factors, the current paper **uses simulation, economic
analysis, and environmental considerations as key criteria.**

The second contribution of this research is to show the importance of
applying discrete event simulation approach in pyrolysis plants'
performance analysis. Most of the existing literature has focused **on
chemical and thermodynamics modeling.** However, there is a dearth of
research that explores other aspects of operation such as productivity,
resource consumption, queue management, and bottlenecking.

Third, the contribution of this research is **the identification of the
key factor behind the efficiency of the process** – the pyrolysis
reactor, which controls product flow, financial benefit, and waste
management capacity of the process.

Fourth, the research contributes to the body of knowledge by developing
a preliminary **economic analysis model** that allows estimating capital
investment cost, operational cost, revenues, payback period, ROI, and
sensitivity analysis.

Finally, this study contributes to the field by providing practical
implementation of **circular economy concepts**, where waste tyres
become products of value.

3.  <span dir="rtl"><span id="_Toc229233566"
    class="anchor"></span></span>**Recommendations for Industry**

Based on the analysis of results, the following recommendations are
applicable in the industry.

**First**, there should be attention paid to improving the performance
of the **reactor** as it appears to be the limiting factor. Reactor
cycle time reduction, improving the processes of loading/unloading, or
installing another reactor would help increase the capacity.

**Second**, there should be maintained an effective buffer/batching
strategy. Due to the fact that the **reactor** **requires** a full batch
to start operating, inappropriate batching will result in waiting,
stacking, and reactor idle time.

**Third**, investments **should not be solely based on optimism
regarding future profits**. A comprehensive market study has to be
performed in order to establish **actual selling prices and demand
levels** of pyrolysis oil, carbon black, steel wires, and light fuel
fraction.

**Fourth**, special attention should be paid to **product** **quality
management**. Economic value of pyrolysis products highly depends on
their quality level, namely pyrolysis oil and carbon black, and further
upgrading/treatment might be necessary.

**Fifth**, the issue of **environmental regulations** should be taken
into account during project planning stage since the costs associated
with emissions, gas treatment, safety equipment, permits, and waste
handling should be estimated and incorporated.

**Sixth**, **the preventive maintenance technique** should be utilized
in order to minimize downtime periods because the reactor is the source
of all production.

**Seventh**, it should be maximally utilized that **pyrolysis gas** can
be used as a heating resource.

4.  <span dir="rtl"><span id="_Toc229233567"
    class="anchor"></span></span>**Future Research Directions**

Future research can extend this study in several directions.

**First**, further work can be done with data **on the operation of
existing facilities** to enhance the accuracy of processing time,
machine capacities, downtime, product yield, and economic analysis.

**Second**, further studies can focus on **creating a process model that
considers such factors** as temperature, residence time, reactor type,
catalyst usage, and composition of tyres as factors influencing the
yield and quality of the final products.

**Third**, future investigations can be carried out to **examine the
performance of discrete-event simulation combined with chemical
processes simulation** tools like Aspen Plus.

**Fourth**, a life cycle assessment could be performed to **evaluate
more precisely the influence of various factors on greenhouse gas
emissions**, energy consumption, avoided emissions, benefits from
materials recovery, and comparisons with alternative treatment
technologies such as landfilling or incineration.

**Fifth**, further research may consider various configurations of
pyrolysis systems involving **the use of two reactors**, increase in
reactor capacity, change in batch sizes, adding extra shredders, and
better buffering strategies.

**Sixth**, uncertainty analysis can be conducted based on the additional
factors **like fluctuations in the price of products, equipment
breakdowns and downtime, inflation, increasing labor costs, higher
maintenance costs, and possible regulatory risks**.

**Seventh**, further investigations could be done into **possibilities
of product upgrading** in order to raise their value and make them more
marketable.

**Finally**, further research may produce a decision-making tool to
choose the optimal configuration of the waste tyre pyrolysis plant based
**on simulation results, economics, and environmental impact**.
